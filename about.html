About

Motivations

With Netflix, Disney+ and various streaming platforms rising, it is crucial for big companies to get a sense of what movies and shows people are interested in so that
they can retain their users. This would also benefit the users since they would be able to access their favorite shows on the streaming platforms, being able to escape
from any emotional stress caused at this time of uncertainty. Through our project, we hope to provide meaningful information and trends regarding films on multiple
streaming platforms for the companies as well as the consumers.

Data Processing

We found several datasets on Kaggle that contain information about TV shows and movies that are on different platforms, such as details about the preference of
different age groups and the countries that created the films. The information that we needed for each of the visualizations were mostly in multiple datasets, so we
processed the data so that we had all the necessary variables in one dataset for each visualizations. For the first visualization which compares the trends of movie
tickets sold and netflix subscriptions, we could not find any dataset related. So for this particular visualization, we manually created a dataset from the statistics
available online.

Design Processes

Our design process for the project entailed a lot of remodeling and improvements. We started out with a sketch of the basic framework of the website and
visualizations, which looked rather different from current form. We made revisions and improvements based on the feedback received along the way. One
method that we found extremely helpful in our design process was the think-aloud study, where we could get honest and unfiltered comments of our project
from the users.

Takeaways

Through this project, we learned the importance of having a storyline in visualizations. At one point in the design process, we only had the graphs sketched
out and did not have any components that would engage the users such as opening or closing statements. We realized that it is important to make the users
understand the message of our project by creating a storyline so that they can follow along and get a sense of our thought processes. We also realized the
power of colors. After incorporating a constant color scheme throughout the website, the visualizations seem to communicate a clearer message.

Team Work

We had a very structured work distribution for the project. We came up with the substructure together and each worked on one of the four visualizations.
Throughout the project, we sought feedback and help from each other regarding the respective visualizations that we were in charge of. After working on
the distributed parts, we came together and proposed ideas on combining the visualizations into a website as a final product. While a member of the team
was working on combining the visualizations, others worked on the analysis part of the project and assisted in designing the final product.
